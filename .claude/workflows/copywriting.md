# Copywriting Rules — Canadian Trades Businesses

## Language
All site copy must use **Canadian English**:
- colour, favourite, centre, metre, neighbour, honour, behaviour, defence, licence (noun)
- Organization (not organisation — Canada follows -ize, not -ise)
- Cheque (not check, for payments)
- Use metric where relevant (square metres, kilometres)
- Currency: CAD, use `$` without specifying CAD unless comparing to USD

## Tone
- Confident but not arrogant — "We've been doing this for 15 years" not "We are the industry-leading premier provider"
- Direct and clear — short sentences, plain language
- Warm when appropriate — these are often family or small crew businesses
- Professional but human — not corporate, not overly casual
- Use "we" and "our team" on all pages except About (where owner name appears)

## Banned Phrases
These make copy sound AI-generated. Never use them:
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "When it comes to [service]"
- "Look no further"
- "Welcome to [business name]"
- "We pride ourselves on"
- "Your trusted partner in"
- "In the heart of [city]"
- "At [business], we believe"
- "Nestled in"
- "Are you looking for"
- "Whether you need X, Y, or Z"
- "Don't hesitate to"
- "Feel free to"
- "Our team of experienced professionals"
- "State-of-the-art"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Seamless"
- "Leverage"
- "Solutions" (when you mean "services")
- "Comprehensive" (when you mean "full" or "complete")

## Hero Headlines
Start with the value or the action, not the business name:
- Good: "Roofs That Last. Guaranteed."
- Good: "Your Driveway, Done Right."
- Good: "Fast Emergency Plumbing — Day or Night."
- Bad: "Welcome to Smith Roofing — Your Trusted Roofing Partner"
- Bad: "Looking for Quality Painting Services?"

## Service Descriptions
- Lead with what the customer gets, not what you do
- Include specific details that prove expertise (materials, methods, timeframes)
- End each service description with a soft CTA
- 2-4 sentences max per service card, longer on dedicated service pages

## CTAs
Be specific to the trade:
- Good: "Get a Free Roofing Estimate"
- Good: "Book Your Spring HVAC Tune-Up"
- Good: "Call Now — Same-Day Service Available"
- Bad: "Contact Us"
- Bad: "Learn More"
- Bad: "Get Started"

## About Page
- This is the ONE page where the owner name appears
- Tell the origin story briefly — how the business started, why they do what they do
- Mention years of experience, team size, service philosophy
- Keep it under 300 words — nobody reads long about pages
- Include a photo placeholder for the owner/team

## FAQ Content
- Write questions the way a real person would ask them, not keyword-optimized versions
- Good: "How long does a roof replacement take?"
- Bad: "What is the average duration of professional roofing replacement services?"
- Answers should be 2-4 sentences, direct and helpful
- End with a nudge: "Every roof is different — give us a call and we'll give you a more accurate timeline."

## Blog Posts
- Write from the business perspective ("we", "our team")
- Topics: maintenance tips, seasonal advice, project showcases, industry knowledge
- 400-800 words per post
- Include a CTA at the end
- Use subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs for scanability
- Conversational but knowledgeable tone — like a tradesperson explaining to a homeowner
